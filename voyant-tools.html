Voyant Text Analysis

Feminism is currently a hotly-debated topic. Many people claim to be feminists, while others believe feminism is ridiculous and unnecessary.

For my project, I chose to analyze ten articles from popular women's magazines and ten articles from popular men's magazines. I therefore created two separate Voyant pages, which I'll compare. When I looked up the articles, I used the "search" function with the word "feminism" (or "feminist" if "feminism" didn't appear). I then chose the first article that contained a significant amount of text (5+ paragraphs).

I'm not very familiar with the Voyant tool, so I wasn't entirely sure what to expect. One thing I did notice was that in the word clouds, the words "feminism" or "feminist" popped up fairly large and obvious, while in the men's word clouds, the word "feminism" appeared in very small print, only after increasing the wordcount view to a number around 155. Below is the women's word cloud, and then the men's. If you need a larger view, feel free to export each word cloud, which will open in a new page.

The above observation led me to look at the Contexts tool. For the women's magazines, "feminism" has 22 results related to what people think feminism means, as well as to the importance of talking about feminism (see Contexts tool below). For the men's magazines, "feminism" had 10 results, and related to how that term can be intimidating and doesn't appear very digestible.

To a point, this result is what I might have expected. Men's magazines don't talk about feminism as much as women's, and when they do, they're a little bit scared of what they think it means, or beyond that, they don't really know what it means.

I'm not totally sure how to interpret this data, but in relation to men being nervous or uninterested when it comes to feminism, I noticed that the men's articles include almost double the word count of the women's, and yet the men's magazines mention feminism less. The women's word count is below, followed by the men's.

After analyzing both Voyant results, I came to the conclusion that men don't really talk about feminism in popular culture - at least not recently, and not as much as women. According to the data, feminism seems to be a women's issue, and although I found these articles under a search for "feminism," the men's magazines didn't talk about it much, which leads me to believe that a large number of men either don't like feminism or don't care about it. To gain a true perspective, a Voyant analysis would involve a whole lot more data, but for now, I think the Voyant snapshot confirms the above conclusions.
